Iran under the rule of the Islamic Republic

The regime frequently targets Iranian activists and religious and ethnic minorities for peaceful civic activities and the exercise of freedom of expression and belief.

Currently, there are more than 800 prisoners of conscience detained in Iran.

Since the Iranian regime came to power in 1979, Iran has conducted terrorist plots, assassinations, and attacks in more than 20 countries worldwide.

In July 2018, an Iranian agent was caught in a foiled terror plot on Iranian dissidents in Paris.

Iran’s development and proliferation of ballistic missiles poses a critical threat to regional security and remains a significant challenge to global nonproliferation efforts.

In 2010, the UN Security Council placed tough limitations on Iran's ballistic missiles program, recognizing that such missiles would likely be Iran's preferred method of delivering a warhead in the future.

The Islamic Republic regularly uses shell companies to illicitly finance U.S.-designated terrorist groups like Hizballah and Hamas.

Since 2012, Iran has spent over $16 billion propping up the Assad regime in Syria.

The Islamic Republic poses a major threat to freedom of navigation and maritime security from the Persian Gulf to the Red Sea.

The Iranian Navy has overstepped its power in the region's waterways consistently--holding international naval crews captive, firing shots at cargo ships, and causing near collisions and other endangerments.

The Iranian people feel the pain of the regime’s environmental mismanagement in many ways, but limited access to water is among the most pronounced and affects approximately 96 percent of the country.

According to a 2017 report by the United Nations, water shortages in Iran are so acute that agricultural livelihoods are no longer sustainable.

The Islamic Republic is a leading threat actor in cyberspace. It has developed cyber capabilities with the intent to surveil and sabotage its adversaries and threaten international stability.

Over the past decade, public reporting indicates that the Iranian regime has conducted cyber operations targeting governments and commercial and civil society entities in the U.S., Israel, Saudi Arabia, and Qatar, among others.
